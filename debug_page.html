Three Stages of Love

EeveesCloset

Summary:

Valentine's Day. The season of friends, family, and an unwavering love that binds us all. Follow Kloud, a go-lucky Umbreon; Saphero, a nerd-loving Espeon; and Solus, the fun-loving Riolu that captures it all.

Notes:

Hiya! Just a quick A/N:

I made this submission a few months back for a Valentine's Day writing competition. It features characters from my universe, Between Two Worlds, and serves as a fun tribute.

While older, this one-shot holds a special place in my heart. I wish you all a fun reading experience, and happy (belated) day of love! Cheers! ^w^

Work Text:

Bzzt!

Kloud stifled a yawn as he slid the glass doors wide. Cherry-blossom conditioner fluffed the Umbreon's fur, fresh steam emerging from the bathroom as he dried his face.

Midnight showers. The best kind. They were cozy, warm, and the perfect way to end a long week of traveling and boredom. Kloud drooped a towel over his bedsheets, crawling in and pulling a soft pillow for support.

He closed his eyes.

Finally. The world of sleep called to him—so soft, so gentle. His mouth closed, lips smiling, and his mind began to drift...

Bzzt!

Or not. A tired eye peeked open.

One unread message.

"Ugh..." Kloud rolled over on his back. The phone unlocked after a quick swipe, and he squinted at the screen.

...

One notification from Saphero. At midnight?

Kloud, you awake?

"It's way past his bedtime..." Kloud hummed, shaking a drop of water from his paws. He typed a considerate and thoughtful reply into the chatbox.

sup.

Beautiful. The period added emphasis. Saphero's blurry icon jumped online immediately, and Kloud watched the three bubbles bounce.

Are you available to chat? :D

Sleeping was no longer an option. He sat up, tossing the wet towel in a pile on the floor. He'd pick it up later—probably. The Umbreon sent a gif of a sleeping Eevee and replied with haste.

abt whtt? y r u awak?

Kloud glanced around the room. Quiet, dark, peaceful; just the way he liked it. The whistle of wind against his window drew his mind to the outdoors, and ebony paws parted the floor to get a closer look.

Full moon. The night slept in a blanket of stars, dancing about without a cloud in the sky. If only he weren't so exhausted, he'd lay outside now. Take a little nap in the field... relax in the chilly wind.

He licked his lips at the thought. If only. His phone gave a little cry from the bed, ending the tender moment.

It's a secret, Kloud ^w^ This is confidential information—top-secret intel! You must promise not to tell a soul...

Kloud rolled his eyes. Oh, Saphy. Always so dramatic. He wouldn't rat his friend out in a million years.

k ip romiss e <3 nerrd

Auto-correct couldn't save him. Reading Kloud's messages was like cracking a jigsaw puzzle, but Saphero had experience.

The Espeon's icon danced for several long seconds...

Just to clarify: you're not too tired, right? I don't wanna keep you up!

Was Saphero stalling? Just get on with it already.

na h its only middnight

Kloud snapped a quick picture of his face. He held up a peace sign, wet and fluffy fur lit by the glow of his rings.

Saphero replied right away.

Wait, did you just get out of the shower? Your fur looks wet! It's awfully late for that, Kloud?

Kloud laughed to himself.

u left ur sham poo n condishoner here LOL!

He sent another picture of himself. This time, he flipped the camera off with a claw, winking as the picture flashed. Perfect. He set his phone down and waited.

Ten seconds. Thirty-five.

When his phone buzzed again, he grabbed it with giddy paws. He assumed it would be Saphero, but when his eyes found the new notification...

...

Kloud hissed at the screen. "Damn dating apps," he groaned. A Weavile girl he followed from this morning just swiped him. She was already typing up a storm, and judging by the time of night and proximity to Valentine's Day, Kloud had a feeling he knew what to expect when he opened the newest message.

Lookin' for some fun tonight, cutie? 1843 Rosebush Street, fourth floor room three. Can send XXX pics <3

The Umbreon sighed. He needed to delete those apps. At least this time it had only been a message.

A new notification vibrated his phone. Saphero's inbox sparkled with a new message.

Hehe, what was that for? Don't flip me off, you freakin' meanie! :D

"Freakin'..." Kloud repeated the words with a chuckle. Saphero was so innocent it hurt. Small, cute, adorable little nerd, that Espeon was. Kloud responded with a dumb emoji and a short message.

stop stalling n tel me the tea saph~

The forsaken tilde. Kloud's secret weapon. Golden rings blinked with mischief, an evil grin parting his lips. He watched the screen jitter.

Actually, Kloud, why don't you come over instead? I'm alone tonight, and a sleepover sounds fun! I can tell you ALL the tea over a bag of popcorn! ^w^

A sleepover? They weren't five anymore. Kloud chuckled and adjusted his pillow.

whtt. u mis me alrdy saphy?

There was a short pause. The Umbreon nearly sent another pic of himself—because why not—but Saphero's newest message caught him off guard.

He read the text out loud.

Comeee on, please? :3 I wanna sleep with you, Kloud! For Valentine's Day!

...

No. The dating apps were corrupting his brain. Saphero was his best friend—a dainty, wholesome scientist! Kloud was overthinking it.

' Bzzt, bzzt!'

His heart jumped. It was the Weavile girl again. He clicked the pop-up without thinking...

...

324123.png

Well, she wasn't lying. That was a picture all right. He blocked the number immediately.

Ten seconds later, a new message from Saphero appeared.

I promise we won't get TOO messy, Kloud! Since you just took a shower! HAHA! You can be on top, and I'll be on bottom! :3

Kloud stared at the screen. Crimson-colored eyes blinked in disbelief... then he smirked.

His paws reached for the call button.

Ring, ring, ring.. .

A warm, high-pitched voice answered after a small delay. The Espeon sang into the phone mic, not at all tired.

"Oh, hehe! Hiya, Kloud—"

"Saphero, are you asking me for a one-night stand?" Kloud jumped straight to the point. There was a not-so-subtle purr in his voice.

The Espeon on the other line was silent—until he wasn't.

"A-ARCEUS, KLOUD, NO! T-THAT'S NOT...!" Saphero screamed into the phone.

Kloud's smirk grew. That answered that. He listened to Saphero squeal in embarrassment.

"—a-and by sleep with me, I strictly meant platonically! Just cuddling and stuff... BUT NOT THAT KIND OF CUDDLING! The cuddling we always do, WHICH IS PLATONIC—"

"And you want me on top, yeah?"

"...I know what you're doing, Kloud," Saphero whined.

"Hey, you suggested it," Kloud hummed. He flicked his tail off the bed, plopping on his feet. "So, uh, you enjoy being on the bottom?"

"Kloud!" He could hear the Espeon whimper through the phone.

"Again, you said it—"

"Are you coming over or not?" Saphero huffed.

Kloud stretched his limbs, riding the suspense. Should he come over? Implications aside, it was Friday night... they both had the weekend off.

And he liked hanging out with Saphero. Even if it meant dragging his tired paws down ten minutes of cold concrete to get to the Espeon's house.

He shrugged his shoulders and yawned. "Damn, Saphy. Aren't you gonna give me a hint, first? About the tea—or whatever's keeping you up past nine o'clock?"

There was a brief pause. "I'll have you know, Kloud, I stay up later on weekends."

"What? Nine-thirty? Ten?"

"...Sometimes I make it to ten-thirty, actually..." Saphero and Kloud both snickered. Kloud threw his scarf around his neck, waiting by the door. "But, if you must know: it's about Valentine's Day. And the museum tomorrow—today! I may or may not be meeting someone there..."

Kloud paused. His hand was on the knob, the door ever-so-slightly cracked to let a gust of wind through. Did he hear that right?

"You got a date tomorrow, Saph?"

"...maybe..."

Kloud smirked. He threw the door open, slamming it closed behind him. "Arceus above, Saphero! Save it! I'll be over in five!"

Saphero's happy trill was infectious. "Hehe, will do! I'll leave the door unlocked—we can just lay in my bed and watch a movie or something!"

Kloud halted on the first step. He flipped the phone on speaker, scanning the long street. He winked at the night sky. "Before I leave..."

"Mmm?" the Espeon hummed.

"That's a 'no' on the condoms, right?"

One, two, three...

"K-KLOUD—" He hung up the phone instantly. He tucked it in his fluffy scarf, pacing himself down the stairs and into the chilly night.

...

Concrete turned into dirt, and dirt into wet grass. Quite annoying, given that he'd taken a shower only minutes before. No matter. Energy coursed through Kloud's bones as he knocked on the Espeon's door.

'Creak...'

A pink face popped out. Bright, lilac eyes scanned him happily.

"Kloud! Hehe, that was fast," Saphero sang with a giggle. He stepped aside, gesturing his tail forward. "Come on in! I made popcorn—two bags! Extra yummy, too!"

Kloud smirked. He liked the sound of that. Dark paws breached the den moments later, and Kloud bumped their noses together before closing the door with his tail.

He inhaled the sweet scent of olive, lavender, and Saphero: three of his favorite things.

"Just make yourself comfy," Saphero said, sneaking up beside him. "It's been, like, what? A week since you've been here?"

Kloud snickered. He jabbed his friend with a paw. "Guess you could say that."

He knew this den like the back of his claw. Kloud had been here hundreds—maybe thousands—of times. True to his expertise, many of Saphero's walls were lined with boring, thick-covered science books. Mainly physics, some math, a handful of chemistry...

...

"Is that a romance novel?" He pointed at a book on the table. Before he could say another word, Saphero squeaked and rushed to swipe it away.

"N-No, Kloud, it's not!" His voice sounded super convincing. "M-Maybe, um, you should go hang your scarf up in my room? Wait for me in there...?"

Kloud stifled a laugh. Saphero levitated the book behind his body, trying to hide it. Kloud still caught a glimpse of the cover—two boys. A Jolteon and Vaporeon.

Good for you, Saphero.

"Sure. I'll pick a movie out—maybe something romantic? Since you're into that now," Kloud teased. Saphero offered him no response.

Poor Saphero.

After a few pleading looks, Kloud spared him and strolled down the hallway. Saphero's room was the only part of his house that wasn't boring; there were even a few posters on the wall. Cool posters, too. Not boring science quotes. Kloud plopped on the bed, careful not to knock over a candle burning on the nightstand.

"Always so clean," Kloud remarked with a smile. Saphero's room was spotless. Not a stain on the tiled floor. Every notebook, pencil, and figurine was neatly organized—every scarf tucked away, towel folded. It felt overkill. But that was how Saphero preferred things: clean, tidy, and perfect.

Kloud threw his scarf to the side, spotting a thin remote at the foot of the bed. He reached down and grabbed it. "What to watch..."

The first thing that popped up was—unsurprisingly—science documentaries. Yeah, not tonight. Kloud scrolled until he found the fun channels, hovering between an action movie and a random 'Christmas in February' option.

He opted for Christmas.

Cheesy holiday movies hit harder at midnight. Droopy eyes amplified the dumb plots and forced dialogue. The moment he clicked the channel, Saphero knocked on the door.

"Popcorn time!" the Espeon cheered. He levitated a large bowl beside Kloud, crawling up next to him. The Umbreon made room. "Hope you found something good!"

"I picked out your favorite, Saph. Santa's visiting early this year," Kloud purred. He waited for Saphero to get situated, then wrapped a paw around him. "We're gettin' festive tonight!"

"Oh, is it a Christmas movie? I love Christmas movies!" Saphero giggled. Kloud snickered, pointing at the screen.

"Think this one's about a baker girl and a hotshot model guy. I wonder if they'll fall in love, kiss under the mistletoe, and get stuck in a snowstorm at the end..."

"Shhh! No spoilers, Kloud!" Saphero fake scolded. He made himself comfy, sliding under the sheets and laying his head on Kloud's chest. His favorite spot. "Plus, you forgot about the sappy song at the end. There's always a sappy song..."

"And you always sing it," Kloud teased.

"I do not!" Saphero retorted. "You have no proof."

Their eyes met. Kloud smiled, reaching over and grabbing the popcorn. The angle was awkward, but he managed a small handful. "You want some?" he asked, popping a few kernels down his throat.

Saphero shrugged, watching the screen. "Nah, not yet."

Well, fair enough. More for Kloud. The Umbreon took another handful before returning the bowl.

Then he got comfortable.

...

A little stretch here, casual arm-around-Saphero there, light tug against the covers...

"You really did use my conditioner." Saphero's voice broke the silence. He poked his nose against Kloud's neck, crawling on top of him. The Umbreon was warm. And soft. And smelled like cherry blossom. "How much of it was left?"

"None, now," Kloud joked. "Dumped it all down the drain. Hate that flowery stuff..." He wrapped a paw around Saphero, pulling him up higher...

12:55 a.m.

They stayed like that for a solid half-hour. Saphero to his left, popcorn to his right, and a corny movie in front of him on the big screen. Kloud briefly closed his eyes...

Sleep didn't sound too bad, all things considered. A long day of traveling across Sylva Skies had Kloud counting stars. Unlike Saphero, who worked exclusively in his laboratory performing Arceus-only-knows-what experiments, Kloud was exhausted.

And cuddling the small Espeon didn't help.

"Say, Saph?" Kloud purred. He moved a paw to rub his friend's forehead gem—Saphero's weakness. "Tell me about the new boy. Your date tomorrow?"

Saphero's cheeks tinted. He looked away from the screen and found the Umbreon's eyes. "How did you... How did you know it was a boy?"

"Rhetorical question," Kloud hummed. Not quite the right word, but Saphero knew what he meant. "No offense, bud, but it's pretty obvious. Haven't met a straight scientist in the Amethyst Elpi since I've lived here—so my entire life."

Kloud waited for a response, but Saphero stayed silent. His smirk grew.

"Plus, the book you were reading earlier—"

"A-Ahh, um, yes! It's a boy, Kloud!" Saphero squeaked.

Kloud giggled and rubbed that gem between his pads. Teasing Saphero never got old. "Well, spill it: who's the lucky guy? Is he a scientist, a leader, some rich guy... a defender?"

The million-dollar question. Saphero squirmed his way out of it over text, but there was no running now. The spotlight was on him.

"It's, um..." The Espeon blinked. His tongue clicked nervously. "Promise you won't freak out, okay?"

Kloud's curiosity spiked. He narrowed his eyes and nodded slowly.

"Pinky-promise."

"It's Stix."

Tick, tock. Tick, tock.

Their eyes were inches apart. Kloud stole a glance at the TV...

A Gardevoir and Gallade were kissing under the mistletoe. There was a snowstorm outside, and Kloud could hear the cheesy musical number coming in. Right on cue.

He looked down at Saphero.

"Good for you two."

Silence. Saphero expected something else—hesitation, perhaps? Or flat-out anger. But Kloud looked supportive, offering his chest for a closer snuggle.

Saphero happily accepted the offer.

"Really? You mean, you're okay with it, Kloud?" Lilac-shaded eyes stared happily into the Umbreon's. That smile was contagious.

Kloud smirked, nodding his head. "I mean, he's alright with me. I'm over my beef with that Jolteon—and he's over stealing things. So, I see no problem with it."

"Yay!!" Saphero squeaked. Kloud tried to brace himself, but he was too slow.

Hug. Squeeze. Smooch.

Saphero wrapped his entire body around him, squeezing their chests together. Kloud purred—he couldn't help himself. He hugged the smaller Eeveelution, patting him on the back.

"Y-Yeah, buddy. I'm happy for you..." Kloud whispered. Saphero was always a hugger, but this felt excessive. He carefully cleared his throat. "Of course, I'm gonna keep my eye on him. Can't say I trust him yet, but if you're serious about him..."

"Oh, yeah! I like him a lot! He's been helping me in my lab for a month now; kind of like my assistant! He's not so cold once you get to know him," Saphero chimed. He finally let go of Kloud, letting his best friend breathe.

Kloud took some much-needed breaths.

The song from the movie drowned the room. Kloud had a lot more questions he wanted to ask—most of them funny, some serious—but sleep was calling to him. Saphero didn't seem too far off, either.

One particular thought plagued his mind, though. A question that he hoped Saphero would answer. He waited for the right moment.

"Saph?"

"Mmm?"

The music faded. A mini commercial flashed the screen—a Sylveon advertising some holiday candles. Yep, they were on the Christmas channel alright.

Kloud slowly sat up, and Saphero rolled off to the side. The Umbreon's face dimmed, a wave of seriousness clouding his eyes.

"Just so we're clear..." Kloud started. "A new boyfriend. Another guy in your life. While I'm happy for you, I just wanna make sure that we're—"

"Kloud." Saphero smiled at him. He knew exactly where this was going. "You'll always be number one in my life. Forever and always."

Forever and always.

The simple words caught him off guard. Kloud's heart skipped a beat, and he watched his friend with silent eyes.

Tick, tock. Tick, tock.

Saphero rolled over to blow out the candle, flicking the night lamp off with a single switch. The only light remaining was the bright TV—and, of course, the faint glow of Kloud's rings.

Finally, the Umbreon smiled. Warmth flooded his fangs.

"I love you too, bud."

They locked eyes. Saphero peeked at him expectantly, and Kloud eventually moved to settle in beside him.

...

The moment his head hit the pillow, pink paws were against his chest. Then a little head against his neck, tucking itself comfortably. He allowed it—encouraged it, even—and gently wrapped his paws around Saphero.

...

Two minutes. He waited for the Espeon's breathing to slow, that pink fur to still. When a warm, gentle breath hit his neck, Kloud whispered a quiet promise:

"Forever and always..."

Valentine's Day is about: friendship.


11:55 a.m.

Five minutes before the next match started. Solus sat with Inferno and Pixie, sipping a bottle of water and adjusting his gloves. The current game heated up in its final stretch.

Tied score. Ninety seconds on the clock. A pass from right field ricocheted off the walls, landing at the feet of a Raboot.

The star of the show.

The bunny ran down the field, maneuvering through two defenders and flicking the ball over a third. He had eyes on the goal.

Dribble. Aim. Shoot!

The ball whistled and hurled toward the net. Eighty seconds on the clock. The goalie made a dive for the top left corner...

...

"GOAL!" Pixie cheered! "Woohoo! Go, Dragons!" She jumped in her seat, forming an exclamation point with her ribbons. Typical Pixie. Cheers erupted around the small stadium, and the enemy team quickly reset the ball back to the center.

The game was lost and they knew it.

Solus took a deep breath, watching the final minute play out. "Hope I don't have to verse that guy..." the Riolu mumbled. Inferno tilted his head, and Pixie settled back in the bleacher.

The Blaziken and Sylveon looked at him from both sides.

"Pfft! That guy's got nothin' on you, bud!" Inferno said with a grin. He flashed Solus a thumbs-up. "You're the best goalie in the league!"

Well, that wasn't true. But Solus appreciated the pep talk.

"Yeah, Solus! You're the bestest in the whole world!" Pixie sang. Sky blue eyes looked at the Riolu warmly. "And the cutest, too! Go kick some Wild Cats' ass—tails! Go kick some Wild Cats' tails!"

Warmth flooded Solus' cheeks. Pixie hugged him with a ribbon, planting a soft smooch on his forehead. The final whistle blew, and the Raboots' team—The Dragons—threw their arms up in victory.

"Another Dragons victory! Final Score: 5-4!"

"You want me to walk with you, Solus?" Inferno asked. The Blaziken wrapped an arm around him, his voice soft. "So you don't have to walk alone?"

Solus thought about it. He could see his teammates—all five of them, hunched up in a circle kicking a soccer ball between one another. The Comets consisted of a Leafeon, Quilava, Sneasel, Charmeleon, Sylveon, and himself.

Anxiety prevented him from warming up with them.

"Nah, it's alright. Wouldn't want you to lose your seat..." Solus whispered.

"Nonsense! It'll be fine," Inferno replied. He stood up and stretched an arm; he wasn't taking no for an answer.

"Yeah! We can all walk together," Pixie chimed. She jumped next to Inferno, motioning Solus forward with her feelers. "Our seats will be fine! Don't worry about us!"

Solus shared a look between them...

They were beaming at him. Faces bright, arms and feelers extended. Inferno and Pixie always did this...

Always supported him. Made sure that he was okay.

Why not?

He rose from the bleachers, clutching his bottle of water and left glove. He jumped on Inferno's shoulders—his favorite spot—and down the trio went.

"Our next match: The Comets vs. The Wild Cats! Keep that popcorn poppin' and those seats warm for the final game of our Valentine's Day exhibition! The kickoff begins in three minutes!"

The announcer's voice dulled as Solus neared his teammates. The walk was short, and Solus swung himself down with a short hop. A ball quickly rolled up to him, and he stopped it with a foot.

"Sup, Sol!" The Sneasel greeted him. Freya, their center-mid player and team captain. "Ready to beat these clowns once and for all? I want those jerks crawling home in tears—and a seed below us! Number two is ours after today!"

Solus rolled the ball back to her. He smiled and tried to sound convincing.

"Of course! Let's blow them out of the stadium!"

He waved a final goodbye to Pixie and Inferno, giving each a quick hug. Inferno bent down, and Pixie squeezed him between her feelers. They'd be rooting for him alright. They always did.

Solus faced the rest of his team, joining their circle after a short pause. A Leafeon immediately called out to him.

"Solus, hiya!" Auburn, their right defender, waved at him. "Hope you weren't expecting to save any goals today, because I'm not letting anyone slip past me!"

Sixty-five seconds on the clock.

"Ha! That's boring—at least give me one or two?" Solus teased. He passed the ball to the Leafeon, and Auburn promptly swiped it to his left. It fell at the feet of Typhy, their left striker.

The Quilava grinned. "Who cares? Medio and I are scorin' ten goals first half! Everyone knows the best defense is a strong offense!"

He passed the ball off to the Sylveon in question, who stopped it with his head. Medio giggled. "Only ten? I think we aim for twenty! Or thirty—fifty, seventy-eight, one-hundred and eighty-four—"

"Or zero."

The cocky voice interrupted them. A Torracat led a group of five pokémon behind them, smirking as he passed through their circle.

The Wild Cats.

Solus narrowed his eyes. The ball stopped in front of him.

"Can't wait to rub your scrawny asses through the turf."

"Fuck off." Freya stepped in front of the Torracat. She crossed her arms. "Leave us alone, Xeonix. Save it for the game."

The enemy team shared a laugh, and a Buizel shoved Freya aside as he passed her.

Yeah, no. Fuck that. Solus swung his foot back and kicked the ball full speed at the back of the jerk's head.

'THUD!'

The Buizel hissed and held his neck, turning to the Riolu with rage in his eyes. Solus gulped. Maybe he didn't think that through...

...

"You little—"

Auburn and Medio stepped in front of him, claws unsheathed. Solus' heart pounded in his chest. His legs quivered, face pale.

Twenty-five seconds on the clock.

The Torracat, Xeonix, chuckled and stood beside his teammate. He calmed the Buizel down with a swipe of his tail.

"Leave 'em be, Jett. We'll save it for the field." His voice was arrogant. So full of himself. The Torracat smiled, looking first at the Leafeon and Sylveon before glancing past them.

When his eyes finally found Solus, his smirk grew.

"Besides," he started with a chuckle. His teammates funneled behind him. "That one's the orphan. Nobody loves him—isn't that right?"

Solus' heart dropped. His lips turned to ice. The mini jumbotron ticked down, and light blue fists coiled up in fury.

Five seconds on the clock.

Soft, dark paws grazed his shoulder. The Sneasel could feel him trembling, and she kept a hand against him. "Solus..." Freya whispered.

The Riolu didn't respond, staring at the empty field.

"And now, the moment we've all been waiting for! The Comets vs. The Wild Cats! Shall both teams take the field!"

"Rot in hell," Auburn hissed. His eyes were fuming. "How dare you! Fuck all of you!"

The Wild Cats didn't respond, taking the field and turning their backs to him. Freya sighed, rubbing Solus' shoulder before clearing her throat.

It was game time.

"This one's personal, guys. Let's kick their asses."

The Comets loaded the field. They took their positions: Medio, the Sylveon, right-striker; Typhy, the Quilava, left-striker; Freya, the Sneasel, center-mid; Auburn, the Leafeon, right-defender; and Pyro, the Charmeleon, left-defender.

Pyro never said much, but he was damn good at defending.

Solus tapped his gloves together, standing on the goal line. Freya and Xeonix flipped a coin at center field, and after a forced handshake, Freya stepped back.

The Wild Cats had the ball first.

"Ready?"

Solus took a deep breath. Lotus Stadium was the fanciest in Sylva Skies. Open nets lined every wall, and the smooth turf allowed for the ball to glide smoothly.

The whistle blew. Loudspeakers blared for the audience.

"Begin!"

A pass to the Wild Cats' backline. The game commenced.

A Dewott playing center field controlled the ball, passing it down the line to a waiting Xeonix.

Medio was on him. The Sylveon intercepted the pass, knocking into the Torracat and sending them both tumbling. The ball slipped loose.

"Typhy!" Freya shouted. She beat a Luxio to the free ball, sending it down right field to the waiting Quilava. Typhy touched it, sliding the ball and himself through a Wild Cats defender. He rushed down the field.

Freya signaled to him. Typhy passed the ball to her at the 18-yard line, setting the Sneasel up for a clear strike. She launched the ball with her foot.

...

"What a save by Hyste!"

Freya hissed. Just a hair too low. The enemy Floragato held the ball, grinning smugly.

"Better luck next time," he mouthed. The ball was promptly thrown over Freya's head, finding an open Buizel as he took charge in the opposite direction.

The Comet's defenders prepared themselves. Despite being an asshole, Jett was shockingly good at controlling the ball. He swung himself around a retreating Typhy, and he flicked the ball up and over Auburn's head when he broke the half line.

The angle was good enough to take a shot. Solus watched the ball, eyes locked. The Buizel glanced at the goal, taking a shot just shy of the penalty box—

"And a remarkable save by Solus!"

The Riolu clutched the ball to his chest. A calculated dive to the bottom-right corner—not a second too late. The Buizel locked eyes with him, and Solus could see the scoff on his cheeks.

He ignored it, kicking the ball out far down the field.

The game swung back and forth. Thirty minutes of nonstop action. One moment, Solus was bracing for a shot—knees bent and arms spread. The next, he was squinting down the field, praying for an eruption of applause and a point on the scoreboard.

At the ten-minute mark, Typhy and Medio finally find their coordination. A beautiful header pass from Medio set the Quilava up for a bicycle kick into the left corner.

It sank into the goal at lightning speed.

1-0.

Xeonix was visibly pissed, and the Wild Cats ended the half one score down.

Freya led her team to the bench. A short one-minute pep talk was in session.

"Good shit out there. Let's put some more points on the board—bury their asses! Typhy, I could kiss you after that shot," Freya purred.

"Same here!" Auburn piped up. The Leafeon chugged his water down, splashing a bit on his face. "Where'd you learn that trick?"

Typhy blushed. "H-Haha, well, Medio set me up... It was really just spur of the moment."

"Well, do it again. We need it," Freya instructed. Her face mellowed as she looked at Solus. The Riolu was gazing absently at the field—he barely heard her speaking to him.

"And Solus. Nice saves out there. Keep your eye on that ball—we're counting on you."

Solus blinked. All five of his teammates were looking at him—even Pyro, who usually sat alone during halftime pep talks. The spotlight was on him.

"T-Thanks, I'll try..."

"And don't let that asshole get to you!" Auburn said with a huff. "We all love you, bud! He's the one that nobody loves—"

"May both teams take the field again!"

"What the hell..." Auburn mumbled. He hated being interrupted. Solus smiled, flashing him a little thumbs up.

"It's alright. Thanks, Auburn."

...

When the half-time whistle blew, The Comets started with the ball. Medio sent a pass back to Freya, who launched the ball to Pyro. The Wild Cats surged forward.

"Back here!" Auburn called. The Charmeleon swiped the ball his way. Xeonix nearly intercepted the pass, but Auburn tapped the ball through the Torracat's legs—sending the fire-type plummeting into the rough turf.

Served him right.

The moment Auburn launched the ball downfield, he turned and laughed.

"Damn. How's that turf taste, jackass?" The Leafeon smirked ear-to-ear.

Xeonix stood up. His eyes ignited in flames.

"That's it..." He lunged at Auburn. The two tussled on the ground, and a whistle was promptly blown.

"Foul! Center-forward Xeonix and right-back Auburn!"

A Machamp charged on the field and pulled the duo apart. A nasty scratch drew blood on Auburn's cheek, but the Leafeon smiled through it.

He held his head high. "Loser!"

"Fuck you!" Xeonix retorted. Jett, the Buizel, ran to his side and crossed his arms.

The water-type whispered something in the Torracat's ear, and Xeonix nodded briefly as he and Auburn were led to the penalty booth.

For the next five minutes, the game would be a 5-on-5.

"Begin!"

A free kick was taken by Freya, who sent the ball high and down left field. It soared over a Wild Cats defender, bouncing off the back wall and ricocheting into Medio's paws. A Jolteon charged at him, but he slid the ball back to Freya.

Dribble. Aim. Shoot!

Freya sunk the ball past the net. A shot from half-field. Solus rushed down the field to congratulate her, and the stadium roared in excitement.

2-0!

"Hell yeah!" the Sneasel cheered. That felt good. She locked eyes with Jett as they passed, smirking ear-to-ear. Solus was right behind her, but the moment he crossed the half-line, a nasty snarl called out to him.

Poison laced the water-type's words. Bitter, heartless.

"Least I have parents, kid."

Freya froze. Solus tried to keep walking, pretending to not hear it, but the Sneasel stopped him.

Her stare was colder than ice.

"I dare you to say that again."

Solus reached out to stop her, but she shrugged him off. Freya waited for the Buizel to say something—to move, breathe, or dare another twisted comment.

But he stayed silent.

The Sneasel snorted, grabbing Solus' arm and leading him down the field. Solus followed along without a word.

Freya. His team captain. Sticking up for him like that...

"Thank you, Freya—"

"No. Don't mention it. You're part of the team."

As simple as that. He wandered back to the goal line, and before the next kick-off started, he stole a glance at the bleachers...

...

When he locked eyes with Inferno and Pixie, they waved at him happily. Pixie created a little heart in the air with her ribbons, and Inferno flashed him a warm smile.

His chest tightened. Suddenly, he felt like crying—but not here. Not now. He waved a shaky, happy paw at them before focusing back on the game.

...

"Ten minutes remaining! What an intense half!"

They held the Wild Cats down 2-0 until the final ten minutes. Auburn and Xeonix were back in the game, and the two held fierce fights for the ball every time it sank into The Comets half.

When the enemy Dewott sent a strike from ten yards out, Solus made the best save of his life. A pile-drive to the top corner—caught, right on the goal line.

Auburn rushed up to him the moment Solus kicked the ball back down the field.

"Holy wow!" the Leafeon gasped. He stared at Solus in disbelief. "You're a legend, dude! How'd you save that?!"

Solus shrugged, equally in shock. He guessed top-right by sheer accident...

Today must be his lucky day.

"Focus on the game, Auburn!" A voice from the audience called out. It was his dad's voice. Auburn's cheeks burned, and the Leafeon awkwardly giggled and trotted back into position.

Five minutes remaining!

The game was looking to be a shutout. Medio and Typhy kept the Wild Cats' defense busy, sending shot after shot into the hands of Hyste.

But that was fine. They were already winning. They just needed to run down the clock.

"Medio!" Typhy called out! He was about to launch a chip pass straight towards the enemy goal, but the ball was tackled from him.

Jett had control of it now. The Buizel dribbled down the field at an alarming pace, zoning in on the Comet's goal...

Pass.

He touched the ball off to Xeonix, who waited until Auburn—predictably—rushed at him to pass it back to Jett. Now it was a one-on-one. Solus, Jett, and the ball closing in on him...

'Wait for it...'

Solus bent his knees low. He focused on the ball...

'Wait...'

Jett was closing in on the ten-yard line. Eight yards. Five. Just before the Buizel sent the ball flying, Solus dove in front of it.

Clasp!

"AND A REMARKABLE DIVE FROM SOLUS!"

He peeked an eye open. He did it. The arena roared as the Riolu clutched the ball in his hands, holding it close. The dive paid off; Solus beat Jett to the ball on a long dribble.

"Way to go, Solus!" Pixie's voice was easy to distinguish from the crowd. Time was still on the clock, but that was easily the last big play the Wild Cats had in them.

They were out of juice, and everyone knew it. Including them.

Solus kicked the ball far down the field, and the Buizel left standing at his goal line—notably without a ball to his feet—cursed and kicked the ground.

"Fuckin' dammit..." A patch of turf came undone as he swung his leg. Solus watched him turn away, pacing with defeated paws back down the field...

Pride swept through his fur. He couldn't help himself, calling out to Jett smugly. "You know. You're an asshole."

The Buizel stopped. His fists balled together—Solus instantly regretted speaking out.

"What'd you say, kid?"

Jett turned, his face cold and bitter. Solus bit his lip...

Two seconds.

The Buizel roared and charged at him, rage in his eyes. Every bone in Solus' body tensed.

He was about to get into a fight. A real fight. His first ever. His legs quivered, and he flinched as the Buizel drew near him...

...

But the pain never came. Movement sounded from his left, and a strong shove sent the taller water-type flying.

'Crash!'

A Charmeleon was standing in front of him. Pyro's body ignited in flames.

"Pathetic..." he growled.

Solus gasped. That was the first time he'd heard Pyro speak all day—maybe ever.

"Foul! Left-defender Pyro, Comets! Yellow card!"

Pyro crossed his arms, shrugging and turning away without a word. Jett was on the ground panting hard, and Pyro voluntarily walked himself to the penalty booth.

...

They won the game 2-0. Freya refused to shake hands, and the team gathered on the bench to celebrate. Another game, another win.

"Heck yeah!" Auburn cheered. Of course, he was the one to pipe up first. "All of you played so awesome! We kicked their butts out there!"

Medio slid in next to him, wrapping a ribbon around the Leafeon. "Yeah, forget those guys! Happy they lost!" The two Eeveelutions smiled. They shared a mini handshake and jumped around on the bench.

Freya crossed her arms, trying to remain stoic. A smirk teased her lips as she purred, "A bit messy, but, we got it done in the end. That's all that matters..." She paused, drawing her eyes to the Charmeleon.

Pyro was sitting on the far end of the bench, silent and staring at nothing. The Sneasel chuckled and moved in front of him.

"And Pyro. Way to stick it to that dipshit—that was awesome!"

The Charmeleon blinked. He grunted, but Solus could sense the hint of a smile on his snout.

"Yeah, well, fuck that guy..." Pyro snorted.

Freya smirked. She patted the Charmeleon on the shoulder, waiting for the rush of adrenaline to dwindle before sitting next to Solus.

Auburn, Medio, and Typhy piled beside her.

"And you, Solus..." she started. Four pairs of eyes landed on him. "You're the best goalie in the league. Hands down."

Hearing it from Freya sounded more convincing than Inferno and Pixie. Heat rose to his cheeks as he looked away coyly, rubbing his arms together.

"Ahh... You really think so?"

"No," Auburn chimed. "I know so! You shut them out, dude! Those saves were next-level!" He slugged Solus on the shoulder. Medio and Freya quickly joined him.

They all shared a hearty giggle, and nobody noticed a Raboot strolling over to them...

The Dragons. The team that played before them. Ranked number one in the league, Garnet was their top player.

When Freya noticed him, she crossed her arms and smiled.

"Stayed to watch our game, too?"

Solus looked over at the Raboot. He remembered him from earlier—the game-winning goal with eighty seconds on the clock. Why was he here now? Did he come to congratulate them?

The Raboot waved his paw, gliding a soccer ball between his feet.

"Thought I'd swing by and say congrats! That was easily the best game of the day—way more physical than ours! And, dude, what a goal!" He grinned at the Quilava and offered his hand.

Typhy blinked. Was he talking about the bicycle kick? The fire-type timidly shook the Raboot's paw, chin high. "Thanks! Really, it was only because of Medio's pass—"

"And Freya! That shot from half-field? Beautiful. Flawless. You better bring that energy Tuesday for our match—all of you!" A playful smirk lined his cheeks. He stilled the ball at his feet, offering a little bow.

Solus stared at him. Garnet was the opposite of what he expected. Arguably the best player in the league, but unlike Jett, Xeonix, and the entire Wild Cats roster, the Raboot was cordial.

Solus flushed when their eyes met.

"And, you, little guy!" Him? Solus blinked... his chest tightened. Garnet's finger singled him out, ruby eyes narrowed. Solus wondered if he'd said something wrong...

Please, not now.

"Damn good saves out there, kid! Some of those shots looked tough, but you made it look easy. I'll have to work double if I wanna score on you!"

Solus blinked. Did he hear that right? It didn't help that Garnet was walking up to him, offering his paw.

...

Shake, shake.

Solus shook hands. He released after a quiet giggle, smiling to himself. Garnet lingered for a few more seconds, staring at the Riolu before tapping the soccer ball between his legs...

Megged.

Dammit.

"We're still gonna win, of course. But it'll be a great match! Just between us, though..." Garnet hummed, keeping his voice low. "I can't stand those guys. Glad you kicked their asses."

He was talking about the Wild Cats, of course. Auburn piped up and stood from the bench.

"Yeah, serves them right! They're a bunch of jerks!"

One by one his teammates agreed. After a few final remarks, as well as a friendly round of keep-up with Garnet, they dispersed.

Freya walked with Garnet out on the field, and the others found their family and friends. Solus slid his way around the oval stadium, eyes locked on the bleachers...

...

"Solus!"

There it was.

Two voices melted his heart. Inferno and Pixie were waiting for him, calling him forward and cheering his name. The moment Solus spotted them, his smile fizzled.

He thought he could hold it together. Thought he could make it to Inferno's den at least. But seeing their faces only replayed the words in his head...

'That one's the orphan. Nobody loves him—isn't that right?'

A tear ran down his cheek.

Without warning, he dropped his water bottle and gloves on the floor. He wiped his face and ran up to them.

Sprint. Sprint. Hug!

They caught the bundle of fur easily, squeezing him tightly.

"Solus...?" Inferno mused. Another tear washed the Blaziken's chest, and he glanced at Pixie before whispering. "Everything alright, bud? That was such an awesome game! We're so proud of you!"

He released the Riolu and waited. Solus wiped his eyes, sniffling before looking between them...

'Least I have parents, kid.'

"I-I... I really, really love you guys..." Solus murmured.

A wave of panic clouded the Sylveon's eyes. She looked at Inferno, then back at Solus.

"Did... Did something happen, Solus?" Pixie whispered. No dramatics. She sat next to the Blaziken, rubbing a ribbon against Solus' cheek—wiping away the next tear that formed. Then another. She pressed her face close. "Solus, we love you too..."

Silence. From the corner of his eyes, he saw a few of his teammates chatting with their families. Medio talking to his sister, Auburn laughing with his dad, Typhy kicking a ball between his two little brothers...

A sense of longing filled his heart. Family . Something he never had—something that was ripped away from him. The announcer's voice filled the stadium, and Solus sniffled before closing his eyes.

"That's all the action today, folks! Meet us back at Lotus Stadium Friday night for the Sparrows vs. the Vipers! Seven o'clock on the dot! You won't want to miss it..."

The Riolu's voice trembled when he spoke again. "A-Are, we..." He choked on his words. He couldn't say it. Not now. Not here.

Inferno held him. "What is it, bud? Are we what...?"

"Are we like a family, too?"

The words leaked out like venom. His volume was no louder than a whisper, a desperate plea for love. Pixie and Inferno stared at him...

Their hearts split into two.

Oh, Solus.

Whatever happened between now and the game must have been serious. They'd never seen him so hurt before—so vulnerable. Inferno tightened his grip on the kid's shoulder, speaking lowly.

"Sol..."

He thought of the right words to say. The perfect answer. He glanced at his mate for help, but he was surprised to see the Sylveon nodding at him.

She trusted him to handle this. But why? What could he possibly—

Oh.

Of course. Speak from the heart... he'd asked himself the same question for years. He knew the answer. He'd known it all along.

"Solus, you've been family to me since the day I met you..." His voice was warm. Loving. The kind of tone a father would have.

Father?

When Solus stayed silent, he continued. "You're the best thing that's ever happened to us. We love you, Solus—I love you. So, so much. More than you could ever imagine..." His voice was a whisper. He extended an arm out.

...

Silence.

The Riolu blinked, rubbing a sore eye. He didn't move.

One second. Three. Five.

Light blue fur buried itself into the Blaziken, squeezing and never letting go. Inferno held Solus close, and a pair of pink ribbons glued them all together in a tight hug.

Pixie planted a kiss on Solus, then another on Inferno. "We'll always be here to support you, Solus! Because that's what families are for!"

She squeezed him tighter.

Shuffling paws and happy chatter blared around them, but their hug never ended. For the first time in Solus' life, that aching feeling in his chest faded.

He was loved. Wanted. Cared about. He'd never let anybody convince him otherwise: Not tomorrow. Not the next day. Not ever again.

When the loudspeakers blared a final goodbye, Solus pulled away. An hour past noon. His fur was ruffled, but he smiled with lips that stretched ear-to-ear.

One last question to ease the tension. The Riolu took a deep, shaky breath, staring at them with heartfelt eyes. 

"Does that mean we can still grab ice cream on the way home? "

"We thank you all for visiting Lotus Stadium! Until next time!"

Valentine's Day is about: family.


The day of love was nearing its end. Kloud tried every excuse to get out of going to Cobalt Museum, but Saphero wouldn't allow it.

The Umbreon gave in after plea number five-hundred and sixty-seven.

"You only have to stay for an hour or two, but I really want you to meet Stix! Just stay for a little while, Kloud? Pretty-please?"

Yeah, sure Saphero. That was over five hours ago.

When the clock struck 11:30 p.m., the museum was ready to close.

And Kloud was ready to go home.

"It was great, guys, but I think this is where I call it..." the Umbreon purred. He was sitting at a table with Saphero, his best friend; Stix, Saphero's date; and Kunai, a Grovyle he'd only met once.

Kloud stifled a yawn. "We'll have to hang out again sometime, though. Go out for drinks—club, party, somewhere that's not a museum? I know a few places, if you're interested." He smirked and offered his hand to the Jolteon across the table.

Stix was balancing an Espeon in his lap, but he maneuvered his paw cleverly over Saphero's head. He shook Kloud's paw with a firm grip.

"You're speakin' my language, Kloud! I could use a drink or two after tonight..." Stix teased. He stole a quick look down at Saphero, rubbing his head. "I've heard enough science facts today to last a lifetime. Isn't that right, Saphero?"

Saphero pouted his cheeks. First it was Kloud, and now Stix? "A museum is exhilarating, and you can never learn enough science...!"

Cricket, cricket.

Bright purple eyes turned desperately to the Grovyle.

"Kunai, back me up!"

"Um..." The Grovyle giggled awkwardly. He scratched his shoulder with a claw, stalling for time. "I mean, I had a good time—"

"See!" Saphero blurted. He turned in Stix's lap, smaller body nice and snug against the Jolteon. "I think you're just a hater! You and Kloud!" He craned his neck to stick his tongue out at his friend...

Kloud smiled and flipped him off.

The speakers in the cafeteria buzzed quietly, bouncing off the walls.

"Attention all guests. Cobalt Museum closes in fifteen minutes. Please clear all exhibits, hallways, and food courts promptly. We open tomorrow at 10:00 a.m. See you then!"

"Well, you heard him," Kloud said with a stretch. He pushed his chair in, grabbing a few wrappers off the table. "I'm outta here! I'll text you guys later this week, maybe. If we're all free?" Before he turned to leave, his eyes locked onto Kunai's.

The Grovyle smirked at him, flashing a discrete thumbs-up.

"I'll walk out with you, Kloud!" Kunai blurted. Without warning, the grass-type stood, pushing his chair in and adjusting his satchel. "I was just about to head out myself. I'll text you later on, Stix. Keep your phone on!" He winked at his friend.

Like Saphero and Kloud, Kunai and Stix were also close pals. Brothers, practically. They grew up together—ride-or-die, through thick and thin. Best friends by every stretch of the word.

And like all good best friends, they knew a good opportunity when they spotted one.

"See ya later, Saph!" Kloud smirked. He followed Kunai out of the cafeteria.

"O-Oh..." Saphero tried to squeak, but it was already too late. The doors closed with a dramatic thud, trapping the two remaining pokémon inside.

Tick, tock. Tick, tock.

Silence. Just him and Stix. Fifteen minutes until the museum closed—the Jolteon's body so intoxicatingly close...

"U-Um..." Saphero whispered. His little cheeks were burning. That bite in his voice faded, replaced with a cute murmur.

Stix enjoyed every second of it.

"Just you and me now," Stix purred with a smirk. His eyes locked onto the scientist. "Bet they did that on purpose. Clever." 

Saphero nodded his head slowly, rolling his eyes. "H-Hah! You're probably right! ...Freakin' those two! What are we gonna do with them?"

The Espeon smiled up at him. Stix didn't respond; he was too busy staring Saphero down.

Saphero. There was a lot to admire. Those soft cheeks, big purple eyes, fluffy fur, cherry blossom perfume...

He paused at the Espeon's lips. Deceivingly soft. Tempting, inviting. He wanted a closer look. A proper taste. He watched the way they trembled under his eyes...

Saphero couldn't take it anymore. "Y-You, uh, can kiss me... if you want..." the Espeon whispered, almost perfectly.

That was a line he'd borrowed from Kloud. One of the many 'tips to get boys riled up!' advice that the Umbreon gave him.

To his relief, it worked.

"Read my mind."

Soft yellow paws reached out, guiding those pink cheeks closer.

Saphero closed his eyes. His heart pounded against his chest—this was it. Only fifteen minutes left before Valentine's Day was over, but not a moment too soon.

When Stix's nose tapped his own, his lips parted automatically. Begging the Jolteon to keep going. Push deeper. Their breathing merged, and warm breath tickled Saphero's chin—it pulled him in.

Smooch

Magical. Even better than Saphero imagined. His face relaxed, lips parting wider as Stix's paws soothed him in all the right ways. He couldn't think. Didn't want to. He just wanted this moment to last forever...

"Cobalt Museum will be closing in five minutes! Please, make your way out of the exhibits, hallways, and food courts. We open tomorrow at 10:00 a.m. See you then!"

All good things had to come to an end, though. When the announcement finished, Stix pulled their lips away...

He smiled at Saphero. "That your first kiss?" Yellow paws rested on pink fur.

The Espeon squeaked. Stix knew the answer already—he could see it in Saphero's eyes. But he wanted to hear it directly. Listen to the words echo from those cherry-pink lips firsthand. 

Saphero flushed and tilted his ears down. "M-Maybe..."

"Cute. You're a natural."

Was that a lie? Saphero couldn't tell—but he hoped it was true. He wanted to kiss him again. And again, and again, and dare he say for a fourth time?

"I-I, uh..." What was the line? Oh, Arceus, what was the line? "I guess you'll have to help me practice more..." Lilac eyes flashed Stix a brave wink.

Another line from Kloud's handout. Just like last time, it worked wonders.

"Oh, is that so? You can practice on me any time, Saphie," Stix purred. He teased the Espeon with a small kiss on his forehead, biting the tuft of fur warmly.

Smooth.

Saphero wasn't ready for that. The cute moan that left his lips was largely involuntary.

"We should probably go, though..." the Jolteon continued. While he enjoyed having an Espeon in his lap, it didn't make getting up very easy. "We can still hang out a bit if you're down. Just not here..."

Saphero quickly caught the hint. He hopped down from Stix's chair, letting the Jolteon free. Before he got ready to leave, though, he checked his phone...

...

There was one new message from Kloud. He gulped, clicking on it with nervous paws...

It was even worse than he feared.

way 2 goooo saph! LOL! #firstkissxxx

Attached to the message was a picture. Kloud and Kunai stood outside the window, taking a selfie with dramatic faces as Saphero and Stix kissed in the background. The quality looked HD—not a blur in that picture.

Every spike of fur on Saphero's body rose. His lungs halted, and he stared at the empty chatbox in disbelief...

...

ARCEUS DAMMIT, KLOUD!
